Using the Google Drive service

Setting up a Google account automatically creates a Gmail address and gives access to Google Cloud services. Google Drive is a sharable file storage service.

Click on the thumbnails to expand the images below.

Once you are signed into your Google account page, a range of services are on offer. Links to these services are displayed with a pop-up group of icons on the top right of the page. If the pop-up is closed, it can be opened by clicking on the grid of nine dots in the far top right of the page.

The Google Drive icon is highlighted in grey in the image below. Double click on the Google Drive icon to access the home page

View the home page. Click on the '+ New' button in the top left to add files to the Drive, or create a new folder.

View your options to create a new folder, upload a file or folder, or create new Google documents on the list.

For example, you can click on the Google Docs option and open a sub-menu that will expand giving the choice of a new blank document or new from template.

A range of templates are shown and can be selected to form the basis of a new document.

A created document can be shared by clicking the 'Share' button in the top right of the page.

Add an email address by typing into the 'Add people and groups' section of the sharing pop-up. Click the 'Done' button. An invite will be sent to invite the person to access the shared item.
